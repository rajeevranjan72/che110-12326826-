    • INTRODUCTION OF FLOOD

    Floods are natural disasters that occur when an excessive amount of water overflows onto normally dry land, often
    causing significant damage to property, infrastructure, and the environment. These events are primarily driven by various
    factors, including heavy rainfall, snowmelt, ice jams, storm surges, or dam failures. Floods can range in scale from loc-
    alized flash floods that develop rapidly to large-scale riverine floods that may take days or weeks to crest.
    The impact of floods can be devastating, leading to loss of life, destruction of homes, disruption of communities, and ec-
    onomic hardship. They are a global phenomenon and can occur in both urban and rural areas. Floods are a recurring and wide-
    spread natural hazard that pose significant challenges for emergency response, disaster management, and long-term resilience.
    This introduction provides a brief overview of what floods are and their general characteristics. Further exploration into
    the causes, consequences, and mitigation strategies associated with floods is crucial for understanding and effectively add-
    ressing the challenges posed by these natural disasters.
